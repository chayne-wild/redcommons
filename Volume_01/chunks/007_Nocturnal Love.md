# Nocturnal Love 

"I must play dark, I must play light, Till bowstrings break my heart outright."

The Fiddler saws the strings, His light brown hair he tosses and flings. He carries a sabre at his side, He wears a pleated habit wide.

II

NOCTURNAL LOVE

Frantic, he holds her near,

Darkly looks in her eye. "Pain so burns you, Dear,

And at my breath you sigh.

"Oh, you have drunk my soul.

Mine is your glow, in truth. My jewel, shine your fill.

Glow, blood of youth."

"Sweetest, so pale your face,

So wondrous strange your words. See, rich in music's grace

The lofty gliding worlds."

"Gliding, dearest, gliding,

Glowing, stars, glowing. Let us go heavenwards riding,

Our souls together flowing."

His voice is muffled, low.

Desperate, he looks about. Glances of crackling flame

His hollow eyes shoot out.

"You have drunk poison, Love.

With me you must away. The sky is dark above,

No more I see the day."



**[p. 24]**

Shuddering, he pulls her close to him.

Death in the breast doth hover. Pain stabs her, piercing deep within,

And eyes are closed forever.

Written in 1837 Printed according to the journal

First published in the Athenäum. Zeitschrift für das gebildete Deutschland, January 23, 1841
