# To My Neighbour Across the Street 



**[p. 541]**

ROMANTICISM À LA MODE

The child who, as you know, once wrote to Goethe, Wanting to make him fancy that he loved her, Went to the theatre one fine day. A Uniform then stalked her way And came towards her with a friendly smile. "Kind Sir, Bettina wishes, for a while, Smitten with sweet desire, to rest Her curly head upon your breast." The Uniform then answered rather drily,

"Bettina, that is up to you entirely!"

"Sweetie," she answered in a trice, "Of course you're sure I have no lice?"

TO THE SUN OF TRUTH (F. QUEDNOW)

Lamplight and star glimmer, Depth of heart and beauty's shimmer,

Soul's grace and white skin's bloom— You never show them openly, Sun of Truth you claim to be.

Every bride has her groom, Sun of Truth you well may call Yourself — the Sun throws shadows, after all!

ON A CERTAIN KNIGHT-HERO

Dig at him here, dig at him there, and ever You'll find that Knight and Hero merge together. His dance-talk's up-to-date all right, But ancient bugs eat him at night.

TO MY NEIGHBOUR ACROSS THE STREET

She stares at me from over yonder; God, I can't stand it any longer. A little man, a yellow house, A woman lank and nauseous. Since Inspiration could take flight, I'd better pull the blind down — tight.
