# Mathematical Wisdom 



**[p. II]**

"You lack the bosom's beat, The heart's life-giving heat,

The soul's high flight so free. The Gods in my breast rule, And I obey them all;

I mean no treachery.

"You shall not captivate Me, nor my love, nor hate,

Nor yet my yearning's glow. It shoots like lightning shafts That gentle power uplifts

In melodies that flow."

The Sirens all sink down Before his blazing frown

In weeping springs of light. They seek to follow him, But ah, the Flood so grim

Engulfs them all from sight.

A PHILISTINE WONDERS

"I don't know how they quarrel with themselves the way

they do. Just button up your coat, good sir, and they won't steal

from you."
