# Medical Student Anthropology 

Damned philistino-medico-student crew, The whole world's just a bag of bones to you. When once you've cooled the blood with Hydrogen, And when you've felt the pulse's throbbing, then You think, "I've done the most I'm able to. Man could be very comfortable, too. How clever of Almighty God to be So very well versed in Anatomy!" And flowers are all instruments to use, When they've been boiled down into herbal brews.

MEDICAL STUDENT PSYCHOLOGY

Who eats a supper of dumplings and noodles, Will suffer from—nightmares, oodles and oodles.

MEDICAL STUDENT METAPHYSICS

No Spirit ever has existed. Oxen have lived and never missed it. The Soul is idle fantasy;

In the stomach it certainly can't be found, And if one were able to run it to ground, Then almost any pill would set it free. Then Spirits would be seen Emerging in an endless stream.

MEDICAL STUDENT ANTHROPOLOGY

He who would sickness foil Must learn to rub his nether half with oil,

So that no wind or draught Can chill him fore and aft. Man also can achieve his ends With dietary regimens;

And Culture thus emerges As soon as Man starts using purges.
