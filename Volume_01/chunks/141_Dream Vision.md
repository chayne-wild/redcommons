# Dream Vision 



**[p. 609]**

That Earth and Heaven remain;

That, as we tremble even,

And worlds within us drown, No tree trunk's ever riven,

No star goes plunging down.

Dead you'd be otherwise,

Your grave the ocean blue, All gone, the shining rays,

And all fire spent in you.

Truth you'd speak silently,

Not dazzle with dead light, Nor shine in clarity;

And all round would be Night.

DREAM VISION

A Dithyramb

From my dreamings I would coax Soft an image in scent-woven web;

I would weave rings passing fair From the locks of my own hair;

Night-encompassed, heart's blood I would swell That, from waves of dream, fire-image well, Image, ebbing and a-flowing,

Fair in love, Aeolian music sighing.

It would soar, all golden shining,

And the little house would arch up higher,

And my locks would wander, curling, Divinest girl in darkness furling,

Forth in pearly songs my blood would flow, Streaming round the marble shoulders' glow, And the lamp would flicker Suns,

My heart would flood Heaven's dome.
