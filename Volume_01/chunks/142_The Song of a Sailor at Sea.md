# The Song of a Sailor at Sea 



**[p. 610]**

In his mighty gaze high festal fire, World-great would be storm's lyre,

Thunder-song my heart would beat amain Suns would be its love and rock its pain, Proudly-humble, I'd sink down,

Proud-audacious, rush unto the breast.

THE SONG OF A SAILOR AT SEA

You may frolic and beat and roll

Round my boat just as you will, You must carry me to my goal; For you are my subjects still.

Blue waves beneath that flow, My little brother's there. You dragged him down below, His bones became your fare.

I was a boy, no more;

Once rashly he cast off, He seized hold of the oar,

Sank by a sandy reef.

I vowed a vow so true

By the waves of the briny sea, I'd be revenged on you,

Lash you relentlessly.

Soul's oath and word I've kept, Them I have not betrayed. I've whipped you and I've whipped, On land have seldom stayed.

When booms the stormy main, The bell rocks in the tower, When blows the hurricane,

When raging winds do roar,
