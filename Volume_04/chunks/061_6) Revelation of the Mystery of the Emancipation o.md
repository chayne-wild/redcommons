OF WOMEN, OR LOUISE MOREL

On the occasion of the arrest of Louise Morel, Rudolph indulges in reflections which he sums up as follows:

Rudolph's reflections do not go so far as to make the servants' condition the object of his most gracious Criticism. Being a petty ruler, he is a great patroniser of servants' conditions. Still less does he go so far as to understand that the general position of women in modern society is inhuman. Faithful in all respects to his previous theory, he deplores only that there is no law which punishes a seducer and links repentance and atonement with ter- rible chastisement.

Rudolph has only to take a look at the existing legislation in other countries. English laws fulfil all his wishes. In their delicacy, which Blackstone so highly praises, they go so far as to declare it a felony to seduce even a prostitute.
