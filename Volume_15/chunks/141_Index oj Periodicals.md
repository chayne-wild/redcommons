# Index oj Periodicals

INDEX OF PERIODICALS

Advertiser—see The Morning Advertiser

Allgemeine Zeitung—a conservative daily founded in 1798; from 1810 to 1882 it was published in Augsburg.— 502

L'Ambigu, variétés atroces et amusantes, journal dans le genre égyptien—a French- language newspaper published in London three times a month from 1803 to 1818.—493

L'Assemblée nationale—a Legitimist daily published in Paris from 1848 to 1857.—116

The Bengal Gazette—see The Calcutta Gazette

The Bombay Courier—an English-language newspaper, organ of the East India Company, founded in 1790; under this title it was published till 1861.— 376

The Bombay Gazette—an English-language daily published under this title from 1791 to 1914.—394

The Bombay Times—an English-language daily published under this title from 1838 to 1861.—318, 319

The Calcutta Gazette—an English-language newspaper published from 1784, official organ of the British Government.— 362

Chronicle—see The Morning Chronicle

Le Constitutionnel—a daily published in Paris from 1815 to 1817 and from 1819 to 1870; the mouthpiece of the monarchist bourgeoisie (Thiers party) during the 1848 revolution; a Bonapartist newspaper after the coup d'état of December 2, 1851.—115, 467, 470, 478, 515, 516, 568

The Continental Review—a weekly published in London in 1858 and 1859.— 480

Courrier Français de Londres.— 493

Daily Courant—a newspaper published in London from 1702 to 1735.—49

The Daily News—a liberal newspaper of the British industrial bourgeoisie; published under this title in London from 1846 to 1930.—163, 343, 345, 373, 388, 516

The Economist. Weekly Commercial Times, Bankers' Gazette, and Railway Monitor: a Political, Literary, and General Newspaper—organ of the big industrial bourgeoisie published in London since 1843.—121, 127, 130, 204, 383, 389, 390, 403, 415, 427, 444, 445, 461, 462, 500-02, 517-21, 560, 562, 564, 577

The Edinburgh Review, or Critical Journal—a liberal literary and political journal published in Edinburgh and London from 1802 to 1929; quarterly in the 1850s.—469

L'Etoile du Danube—a French-language liberal newspaper, published by Romanians in Brussels in 1856-57.—324-26

The Examiner—a liberal weekly published in London from 1808 to 1881.— 247

Le Figaro—a conservative daily published in Paris since 1854 as a continuation of Figaro, Journal nonpolitique that appeared in 1826-33; in the 1850s it took a Bonapartist stand.— 552

77ie Free Press. Journal of the Foreign Affairs Committees—a journal on questions of foreign policy, opposed to the Palmerston government; it was published by David Urquhart and his supporters in London from 1855 to 1865 (weekly till April 1858 and then monthly); it was renamed Diplomatic Review in 1866; it printed several works by Marx.—71, 389, 390, 425, 430, 540, 541

The Friend of China—see The Overland Friend of China

The Friend of India—an English newspaper founded in Serampore (Bengal) in 1818; in the 1850s it appeared once weekly and was liberal in policy.— 374, 376 Gaceta de Madrid—a Spanish newspaper founded in 1661, official organ of the government.—100, 286

Gazette nationale ou Moniteur universel—see Le Moniteur universel

The Globe and Traveller—a daily published in London from 1803 to 1921; mouthpiece of the Whigs until 1866, and later of the Conservatives.— 388, 443

Hamburger Correspondent—see Staats- und Gelehrten-Zeitung des Hamburgischen unpartheyischen Correspondenten

L'Indépendance belge. Journal mondial d'informations politiques et littéraires—a liberal

daily founded in Brussels in 1831.—137, 475, 476, 483

Journal des Chemins de Fer, des Mines et des Travaux Publics—a bourgeois daily published in Paris from 1842.—132

Journal des Débats politiques et littéraires—a daily published in Paris from 1789 to 1944; organ of the moderate Orleanist opposition after the coup d'état of December 2, 1851.—15, 99, 101, 107, 456, 467

The Journal of Psychological Medicine and Mental Pathology—a quarterly published by doctor Forbes Winslow in London from 1848 to 1864; after some interval it appeared till 1883.—599

The Leader—a liberal weekly published in London from March 1850 to June I860.—103

Military Spectator—a weekly published in London in 1857 and 1858.— 374

The Mofussilite—an English-language weekly published in India; under this name it appeared from 1845 to the mid-1870s first in Meerut, then in Agra and Ambala.— 346

Le Moniteur universel—a daily published in Paris from 1789 to 1901 (under this name from 1811); from 1799 to 1869 it was an official government newspaper.—15-16, 18, 22, 23, 97, 118, 119, 122, 123, 130, 131, 133, 272, 273, 275, 276, 391, 413-15, 417, 454-57, 459, 463, 466, 472-75, 477, 478, 480, 482, 483, 490, 491, 493-501, 503, 516, 550, 551

The Morning Advertiser—a daily published in London from 1794 to 1934; in the 1850s it was a newspaper of the radical bourgeoisie.—249-50, 372, 596

The Morning Chronicle—a daily published in London from 1770 to 1862; organ of the Whigs in the 1840s, of the Peelites in the early 1850s, and later of the Conservatives.—388

The Morning Herald—a conservative daily published in London from 1780 to 1869.—104, 388

The Morning Post—a conservative daily published in London from 1772 to 1937; in the mid-nineteenth century it was the mouthpiece of the Right wing of the Whig Party grouped round Palmerston.—238, 247, 317, 328, 388, 596

Die Neue Zeit. Organ der Demokratie—a newspaper of German émigré workers published in London from June 1858 to April 1859.— 589

New-York Daily Tribune—a newspaper founded by Horace Greeley in 1841 and published until 1924; till the mid-1850s it was the mouthpiece of the Left wing of the American Whigs, then of the Republican Party; in the 1840s and 1850s it voiced progressive views and opposed slavery. Marx and Engels contributed to it from August 1851 to March 1862.—3-7, 10-12, 106, 107, 122, 151-55, 168, 207, 324, 360, 384, 541, 546

The New-York Herald—a daily newspaper of the Republican Party published from 1835 to 1924.—517

The North British Daily Mail—a Scottish newspaper founded in Glasgow in 1847, organ of the liberal bourgeoisie.—401

L'Opinione—an Italian daily founded in Turin in 1848; organ of Cavour's liberal monarchist party.—416

The Overland Friend of China—an English official newspaper published in Victoria (Hong Kong) from 1842 to 1859.—209

La Patrie—a daily published in Paris from 1841 to 1866; in 1850 it voiced the views of the Party of Order; after the coup d'état of December 2, 1851 it took a Bonapartist stand.—475, 516, 517, 520, 568

Le Pays. Journal de l'Empire—a daily founded in Paris in 1849, semi-official newspaper of Napoleon Ill's government from 1852 to 1870.— 320

Le Phare de la Loire—a French daily published in Nantes from 1815.—456

The Phoenix—an English-language newspaper published in Calcutta from 1856 to 1861.—446

Le Pouvoir—a Bonapartist daily founded in Paris in 1849; it was published under this title in 1850 and 1851.—516

The Press—a Tory weekly published in London from 1853 to 1866.— 247, 320

La Presse—a daily published in Paris in 1836-66; in the 1850s opposed the Second Empire, later organ of the Bonapartists.—102, 131

Punch, or the London Charivari—a comic weekly, liberal in policy; founded in London in 1841.—220, 472

Le Siècle—a daily published in Paris from 1836 to 1939; in the 1840s it voiced the views of that section of the petty bourgeoisie that demanded moderate constitutional reforms; in the 1850s it was a moderate republican newspaper.— 551

The Somerset County Gazette—a weekly founded in Taunton (Somerset, England) in 1836.—596

Somerset Gazette—see The Somerset County Gazette

Le Spectateur—the title under which the newspaper L'Assemblée nationale was published from September 1857 to January 1858.—456
