# Index of Periodicals 



**[p. 657]**

INDEX OF PERIODICALS

Abend-Zeitung—a literary daily published in Dresden (1817-50) and Leipzig (1851-57).—81, 392

Allgemeine Militär-Zeitung—a newspaper, organ of the German Officers' and Soldiers' Society, published in Darmstadt and Leipzig from 1826 to 1902; Engels contributed to it from 1860 to 1864.—407, 408, 409, 540

The Army and Navy Gazette—a semi-official weekly of the War Ministry published from 1860 to 1921 in London.—462

[^1]: Le Constitutionnel—a daily published in Paris from 1815 to 1817 and from 1819 to 1870; in the 1840s it voiced the views of the monarchist bourgeoisie (Thiers' party) and after the coup d'état of 1851 became a Bonapartist newspaper.—466

[^2]: The Daily Telegraph—a liberal and, from the 1880s, conservative daily published in London from 1855 to 1937, when it merged with The Morning Post and has ever since appeared as The Daily Telegraph and Morning Post.—485, 487

[^3]: Deutsche Reichstags-Zeitung—a newspaper published in Frankfurt am Main from 1848 to 1849; it expressed the views of the Left wing of the Frankfurt National Assembly.— 393

[^4]: Elegante Zeitung—see Zeitung für die elegante Welt

[^5]: Gazette nationale ou le moniteur universel—see Le Moniteur universel

[^6]: Giornale délia provincia Bresciana—an Italian newspaper.— 278

[^7]: Journal général de la Cour et de la Ville—an aristocratie daily published in Paris from September 15, 1789 to August 10, 1792. Until October 30, 1789 it was edited by Brune.—215, 397

[^8]: Der Komet (Comet)—a liberal literary daily published from 1830 to 1848, first in

[^9]: Altenburg and then in Leipzig.— 81, 392





**[p. 658]**

The London Review—a weekly published from 1860 to 1869.—495

Manchester Weekly Express—the name under which the Manchester Weekly Guardian appeared in 1860-62.—429

Le Moniteur—see Le Moniteur universel

Le Moniteur universel—a daily published in Paris from 1789 (under this title from 1811) to 1901, official government organ from 1799 to 1868.—151

Reichstagszeitung—see Deutsche Reichstags-Zeitung

Revue des deux Mondes—a literary and political fortnightly published in Paris since 1829.—427

Sächsische Vaterlands-Blätter—a liberal newspaper published in Dresden and Leipzig from 1840 to 1845.—81, 392

Telegraph—see The Daily Telegraph

The Times—a conservative daily published in London since 1785.—481, 487

Verfassungsfreund—a liberal newspaper published by Robert Blum and Friedrich Steger in Leipzig in 1843; only two issues appeared: the newspaper was banned by the censor.—81, 392

The Volunteer Journal, for Lancashire and Cheshire—a military weekly published in Manchester from August 1860 to March 1862; Engels contributed to it.—409, 476, 504, 514, 522

[^1]: Vorwärts—a popular almanac published by Robert Blum and Friedrich Steger in

[^2]: Leipzig from 1843 to 1847.—81, 392

[^3]: Zeitung für die elegante Welt—a literary liberal newspaper published under various titles in Leipzig from 1801 to I860.—392
