# 6. Karl Marx. Concerning the Arrest of the Members of the Central Committee of the Social- Democratic Workers' Party 



**[p. 271]**

Karl Marx

[CONCERNING THE ARREST OF THE MEMBERS

OF THE CENTRAL COMMITTEE OF THE SOCIAL-DEMOCRATIC WORKERS' PARTY] m

The Central Committee of the German section of the "Interna- tional Workmen's Association" resident at Brunswick issued on the 5th inst. a manifesto to the German working class, calling upon them to prevent the annexation of Alsace and Lorraine, and to bring about an honourable peace with the French Republic.3 Not only has their manifesto been confiscated by the order of the commanding-general, Vogel von Falckenstein, but all the members of the committee, even the unfortunate printer of the document, were arrested and chained like common felons, and sent to Lötzen, in Eastern Prussia.
