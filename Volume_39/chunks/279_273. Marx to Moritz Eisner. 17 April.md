MARX TO ENGELS

IN MANCHESTER

[London,] 16 April [1855]

Dear Engels,

I shall be leaving with my wife for Manchester on Wednesday morning by the parliamentary train.146

Dronke and I have just seen Badinguet crossing Westminster Bridge.644 A monkey in uniform.

Your

K. M.

MARX TO MORITZ ELSNER

[London,] 17 April 1855 28 Dean Street, Soho

Dear Eisner,

I shall be away from London for a few days in company with my wife. Dronke has kindly agreed to carry on with the articles during that time. He will today be sending you a report on Bonaparte's arrival.3 In case I don't see him again, I am writing to you separately.

The 2 enclosed articles'1 constitute the beginning of a polemic against pan-Slavism.609 In my view it is high time that Germany be made seriously aware of the dangers threatening her. You may
