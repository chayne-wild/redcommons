# 216. Marx to Engels. About 22 February 



**[p. 219]**

MARX TO FERDINAND LASSALLE

IN BERLIN

London, 23 February 1859

Dear Lassalle,

I have today sent off the prefacea to Duncker. Perhaps you would be good enough to ensure that I am sent the fee as soon as the manuscript has been printed. I would certainly not write to you about this point had it not become a question brûlanteb due to unforeseen circumstances.

I hope to have a letter from you soon and, notwithstanding your coyness, something about 'conditions in the homeland', or at least the gossip there, this being essential for forming an opinion.

Salut.

Your

K. M.

220

Dear Lassalle,

London, 25 February 1859 9 Grafton Terrace, Maitland Park, Haverstock Hill

It is Engels' intention to publish—anonymously to begin with- short pamphlet entitled Po and Rhine.379
