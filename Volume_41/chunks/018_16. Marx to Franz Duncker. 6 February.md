16

MARX TO FRANZ DUNCKER

IN BERLIN

London, 6 February 1860 Dear Sir,

Would you very kindly arrange to print the enclosed written statement," which I am sending simultaneously to the National- Zeitung and the Publicist (what its politics are I do not know, but it would appear to be widely read over here) in Berlin; likewise, to the Kölnische Zeitung, the Frankfurter Journal, the Hamburg 'Reform' and the Augsburg 'Allgemeine Zeitung'.

I should be much obliged if you would pass the following on to Lassalle:

Time does not permit my replying to him today. The article on Kossuth,b which I sent Szemere in Paris ON THE EXPRESS CONDITION that it be returned immediately, has been in his hands for months now. I shall now hold a pistol to his head — allegorically speaking, OF COURSE.

I should be most grateful if Lassalle would send Vogt's bookc by post direct to Engels at his private address, 6 Thorncliffe Grove,

[^1]: '•' K. Marx, 'To the Editors of the Volks-Zeitung. Declaration'. - b K. Marx, 'Kossuth and Louis Napoleon'. - c C. Vogt, Mein Prozess gegen die Allgemeine Zeitung, Geneva, 1859.

[^2]: 3—558
