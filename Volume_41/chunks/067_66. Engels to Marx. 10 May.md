ENGELS TO MARX

Manchester, 10 May 1860

Dear Moor,

My brotherb left this evening because my mother is seriously ill and sent for him by telegram.

Matters have been pretty well settled with Ermen. My family is leaving capital amounting to £10,000 in the business, which it will have to make over to me when I become a partner. My material position will improve straight away, or at least the percentage of my share. I shall tell you all about this when I come down at Whitsun. Providing, that is, that everything's settled by then, and

[^1]: IN LONDON

[^2]: a Marx's note to the copy of the letter in his notebook. - '' Emil Engels
