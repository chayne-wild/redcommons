MARX TO ENGELS

IN MANCHESTER

[London,] 3 August 1861

Dear Frederick,

This is to inform you in much haste that the £5 has been most gratefully received.

An odious little sheet was sent to me yesterday entitled Thusneldah—by Kinkel and Zerffi, attacking Heintzmann et cie. Rarely have I encountered such abysmally stupid juvenilia.

The worthy Heinrich Bürgers has made a pro-National Associa- tion24 speech at the Gürzenich,351 and there was a general frisson of pleasure when he breathed the name of the noble man of Gotha.'

Handsome William appears to be completely out of his mind. The fool is studying folio volumes on the coronation of Fred- erick I.

I am sending you the Thusnelda herewith. Look after it for me. Also enclosed an article on the COUNTING OUT OF THE HOUSE ON THE

Salut.

Your

K. M.
