ENGELS TO MARX

[Manchester, about 18 May 1862] Dear Moor,

You must excuse my not writing. Never have I been so overrun as I have this week. Austrians, backwoodsmen,412 Frenchmen and, then, today, Borkheim is saddling me with his associé*—a nice prospect! I no longer know whether I'm coming or going. And, on top of it all, one's expected to go drinking with the whole crew and make oneself agreeable. May the devil take the exhibition.344

In the greatest haste,

Your

F. E.
