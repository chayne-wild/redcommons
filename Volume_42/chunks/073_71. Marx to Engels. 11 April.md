# 71. Marx to Engels. 11 April 



**[p. 70]**

MARX TO SOPHIE VON HATZFELDT

IN BERLIN

London, 10 April 1865

My dear Countess,

I shall be obliged if you would hand the copy of the '18th Brumaire' with my marginal amendments, i.e., corrections, to Mr Wilhelm Liebknecht.79

I have read Becker's3 attacks on you with the greatest indignation, and, to judge from letters I have received from people of the most divergent views, the scoundrel has thereby generally done harm not to you, but only to himself. I am in entire agreement with Mr Rüstow that it is incomprehensible that any section of the Association of German Workers could calmly listen to such scandalous talk. The most elementary sense of decency should make such infamy impossible.192

Yours most respectfully

Karl Marx

71

London, 11 April [1865]

Dear FRED,

Returned home some twenty-four hours ago.177 So, for the moment just a few lines to let you know.
