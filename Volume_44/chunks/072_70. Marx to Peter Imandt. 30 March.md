# 70. Marx to Peter Imandt. 30 March 



**[p. 70]**

MARX TO PETER IMANDT

IN DUNDEE

[London,] 30 March 1871

Dear Imandt,

The 'letter' is a product of that trashy Paris paper, the Paris-Journal, in its issues of 14 and 19 March. 71 A statement from me in reply appeared in The Times on 22 March (a notice in small print just after the leading articles).3 That lousy paper has direct connections with the Prussian police. Its rédacteur en chef,h the notorious Henri de Pêne, received two bullets in his body as a reward for his participation in the 'peaceable' procession of the parti de l'ordre.11

Regards to Bourbaki junior.0

Curiously enough the entire reactionary press in France printed the pseudo-letter. To give it more spice, the Paris-Journal published it under the high-faluting title: 'Le Grand Chef (translation of the Stieberian 'Hauptchef'"6) 'de L'Internationale'.

Salut

Your

K. M.
