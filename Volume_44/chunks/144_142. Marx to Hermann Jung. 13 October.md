# 142. Marx to Hermann Jung. 13 October 



**[p. 224]**

£1 I have sent to Rozwadowski. Give from the refugees' money so much to Duru that he can leave the lodgings he now lives in which are too dear considering their miserable state. It would be well, if Duru received money enough to get his things out of the pawning shop. But my opinion is that he should take them not to his present lodgings but depose them in your house and leave his lodgings without paying the rest of his house-rent I He has already paid more than was really due for such a hole.

Give also say £1 to the nouveau venu3 of whom you spoke yesterday.

These expenses—this employment of part of the money remitted to us from the United States—I shall defend so soon as the disposal of those moneys will come before the Council.320

Yours fraternally,

Karl Marx

MARX TO HERMANN JUNG

[London,] 13 October 1871

Dear Jung,

Could I ask you to regard the £1 for Rozwadowski as privately donated to him from me.

As to Duru, he was already gone when your letter arrived. Therefore, if you have already advanced him further sums, I shall inform the Conseil that I shall reimburse you, if the expenditure is not approved.

[^1]: IN LONDON

[^2]: a newcomer
