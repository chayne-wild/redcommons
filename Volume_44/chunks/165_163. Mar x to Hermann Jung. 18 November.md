MARX TO HERMANN JUNG

IN LONDON

[London,] 18 November 1871

Dear Jung,

On second thoughts it seems to me better after all,

1. that you should write to Dilke simply on your own account, without mentioning me.349 * There are reasons why I should not like to see him on this occasion.*

2. not to put Cournet's name forward as early as Tuesday.350 It is in his own interests not to be in too much haste.

Best regards to your dear wife.

Karl Marx
