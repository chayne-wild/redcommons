MARX TO LUDWIG KUGELMANN121

IN HANOVER

[London,] 23 July 1872

Dear Kugelmann,

If nothing happens in between, I shall be at The Hague450 on 2 September and shall be very glad to see you there. I had already sent you the Scissions etc.* 86 but it seems to have been confiscated. I am therefore enclosing a copy in this letter. You must excuse me for not writing more today. I have to send

épreuves* to Paris and am in general overburdened with work.

Your

K. M.
