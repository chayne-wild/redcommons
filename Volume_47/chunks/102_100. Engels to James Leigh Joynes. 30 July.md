To save postage, the ms. can be sent with the proofs in a stiff, strong wrapper; over here mss. and proofs are both charged as book post. They must, however, carry the full amount of stamps, otherwise they won't arrive here.

100

ENGELS TO JAMES LEIGH JOYNES

[London,] 30 July 1884

Dear Sir,

I am sorry I cannot at present give you my consent to a translation of my Entwicklung etc.* for To-Day, as I am bound by a previous en- gagement to another gentleman.b

As to my promise of an article for To-Day, that promise was given to Mr Bax and as far as I know Mr Bax is no longer one of the Editors of To-Day.c

Yours faithfully,

F. E.
