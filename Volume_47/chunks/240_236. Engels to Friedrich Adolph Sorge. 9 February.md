# 236. Engels to Friedrich Adolph Sorge. 9 February 



**[p. 402]**

Here the industrial crisis gets worse instead of better, and people begin to find out more and more that England's industrial monopoly is at an end. And with America, France, Germany for competitors in the world's market, and high duties excluding foreign goods from the markets of other rising industrial countries, it becomes a simple mat- ter of calculation. If one great monopolist industrial country pro- duced a crisis every ten years, what will four such countries produce? Approximatively a crisis in 10/4 years, that is to say practically a crisis without end. Uns kann recht sein.c

Very faithfully yours,

P.W. Rosher363
