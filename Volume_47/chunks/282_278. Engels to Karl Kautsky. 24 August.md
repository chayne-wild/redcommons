# 278. Engels to Karl Kautsky. 24 August

ENGELS TO KARL KAUTSKY 6 ' 6

IN LONDON

[Eastbourne,] 23 August 1886

As you wrote to tell me you would be forwarding my letters, etc., on Saturday, I simply wanted to inform you that I got nothing either on Saturday or yesterday, nor had anything arrived by 7 p. m. today (Monday); I had at least counted on getting the Sozialdemo- krat.

Liebknecht has written to say he will probably be here this week. I shall come up for the day to collect him and shall write beforehand.

Regards to your wife from Nim and

Your

ENGELS TO KARL KAUTSKY

Eastbourne, 24 August 1886 4 Cavendish Place

Dear Kautsky,

I have been completely crushed, smothered, squashed and stulti- fied by your avalanche of letters this morning. I shall have to see how I can manage to answer them. Very many thanks.

Liebknecht has written to say he may set off for London as early as tomorrow, taking the direct route via Flushing—I am expecting more definite word any day; as soon as I know the day he's arriving,
