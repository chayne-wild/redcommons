ENGELS TO LAURA LAFARGUE40

IN PARIS

London, 10 March 1887

My dear Laura,

Pleasant news. The first edition of Capital* 500 copies, is sold with the exception of about 50 copies and the 2nd edition is in preparation. Nearly half the edition, as far as I can calculate, has gone to Americab

and the 2nd edition will still find a good market there unless a piratical edition is brought out which however will not probably be undertaken before the success of the book in America is manifest and moreover it will take some time. As the clichés are there, the 2nd edition will soon be in the market, and on that we shall have 3/9 in every copy instead of 3/- as on the first. It will again be a 30/- edition.58

We saw the article in the Athenaeum57 and Tussy will send you a copy. It is very fortunate that the press begins to speak of the book just

[^1]: a the English edition of Vol. I - b See previous letter
