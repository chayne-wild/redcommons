# 134. Engels to Friedrich Adolph Sorge 11 September 



**[p. 214]**

134

ENGELS T O FRIEDRICH A D O L P H SORGE 2 9 2

IN HOBOKEN

Plattsburg, N. Y., Tuesday, 11 September 1888

Dear Sorge,

Arrived here safely. We leave for the Adirondacks at 1 p.m. sharp, shall return tomorrow evening and then cross the lakes to the Hudson. Back in New York by Saturday* evening, I hope.

Should any letters have arrived for me, would you please forward them to the Narragansatt Hotel, Albany, but they must get there not later than Friday evening.

I hope you received my letter from Montreal.13 Is your throat all right again?

Your

F. Engels

First published in: K. Marx and F. Printed according to the original Engels, Letters to American";, 1848- 1895, New York, 1953

[^1]: Shall we see your sonc in New York before we leave? All of us well and cheerful. Kindest regards from us all to you and your wife.d

[^2]: a 15 September - b See previous letter - c Adolph Sorge - d Katharina Sorge
