# 209. Engel s to Johann Heinrich Wilhelm Dietz. 12 May 



**[p. 209]**

ENGELS TO JOHANN HEINRICH WILHELM DIETZ

IN STUTTGART

London, 12 May 1892

Dear Mr Dietz,

I am in complete agreement with your proposal of the 9th inst.476

I for my part concede you the right, in so far as it is within my com- petence, to print 10,000 copies, in return for which you are to pay me the following fees:

for 2,500 copies M. 500 in the autumn of 1892, after printing; " 2,500 " " 500 in the New Year of 1893; 5,000 " " 1,000 after printing this second half, but not later than 6months after printing has begun.

In view of your agreement with Wigand which is, after all, still very vague, I think you have chosen the right procedure. Whether you now stipulate one edition or decide to do several is your own af- fair. I of course am much more concerned with placing the work be- fore the public again than with the kudos of having numerous edi- tions.

With cordial regards,

Yours,

F. Engels
