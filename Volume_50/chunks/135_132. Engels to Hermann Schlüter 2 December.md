ENGELS TO HERMANN SCHLII TER 302

IN HOBOKEN

No. 2!!

[London,] 2 December 1893 Dear Schlüter

As regards Census I,a I first got a letter from the General Post Office, Washington, saying that, because of the way it was packed, the book must be paid for as a letter. I must either send $10.36c. for this purpose or let them have the sender’s address (I didn’t know either what was in it or who had sent it), or else say whether I wanted it sent per express. I asked for further details, at the same time saying they were at liberty to send it per express, which they did, and I got it ON PAYMENT OF 6/-. When sending me things in future, would you be so good as to put your address

[^1]: a Department of the Interior, Census Office. Compendium of the Eleventh Census: 1890, Parts I- II , Washington, 1892-1894
